Placese to visit in Sri Lanka



Adam's Peak

Adam's Peak, also known as Sri Pada, is a sacred mountain located in central Sri Lanka. Standing tall at a height of 2,243 meters (7,359 feet), it holds significant religious and cultural importance to several different faiths. The peak is famous for the distinct footprint-shaped impression at its summit, which is believed to be sacred and holds spiritual significance for Buddhists, Hindus, Christians, and Muslims alike.
The trek to the summit of Adam's Peak is considered a challenging and rewarding experience. The traditional pilgrimage season begins in December and lasts until May, coinciding with the dry season in Sri Lanka. Pilgrims typically start the ascent in the late evening or early morning, aiming to reach the peak before sunrise. The climb consists of approximately 5,200 steps, which are illuminated by lights and accompanied by vendors selling food, drinks, and religious artifacts.
As pilgrims reach the summit of Adam's Peak, they are greeted with a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. The most iconic sight at the peak is the "Sri Pada" or "Sacred Footprint," a rock formation with a depression resembling a large footprint. According to Buddhist tradition, this footprint is believed to be the imprint of Lord Buddha, while other religious groups associate it with the footprints of Adam, Lord Shiva, or even St. Thomas the Apostle.

Galle Fort

Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the city of Galle, Sri Lanka. Constructed by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later fortified by the Dutch, Galle Fort is a remarkable architectural gem that showcases the rich colonial history of the region. The fort's strategic location on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka made it an important trading port for centuries.
Exploring Galle Fort is like stepping back in time. The fortification walls, which span over 3 kilometers (1.9 miles), enclose a vibrant and bustling neighborhood filled with narrow streets, quaint cafes, boutique shops, and art galleries. Walking along the ramparts offers breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean and the picturesque town of Galle. The fort's architecture is a fascinating blend of European styles, with Dutch and Portuguese influences evident in the buildings, churches, and clock tower.
Galle Fort is not only a historical site but also a cultural hub. The fort's diverse population consists of locals, expatriates, and artists who have transformed the area into a creative enclave. The streets of Galle Fort are lined with art galleries showcasing contemporary and traditional Sri Lankan art, as well as boutique hotels and restaurants offering a fusion of local and international cuisines. The fort also hosts various cultural events throughout the year, including music festivals, literary festivals, and art exhibitions, attracting both locals and tourists.

Gangaramaya Temple

Gangaramaya Temple is a prominent Buddhist temple located in Colombo, the capital city of Sri Lanka. Established in the late 19th century, the temple is a vibrant center of worship, religious education, and cultural activities. It is known for its unique blend of architectural styles, combining traditional Sri Lankan, Indian, Thai, and Chinese elements. The temple complex comprises several buildings, including a main shrine, an assembly hall, a library, a museum, and a residential area for monks. It serves as a gathering place for devotees, tourists, and scholars, attracting visitors from all walks of life.
The temple's richly adorned architecture and intricate artwork make it a visual spectacle. The main shrine houses a large collection of Buddha statues, including a 14-foot-high seated Buddha statue made of brass. The walls are adorned with murals depicting significant events from the life of the Buddha. The assembly hall, known as the "Seema Malaka," is a floating platform built on Beira Lake, offering a serene and picturesque setting for meditation and contemplation.
In addition to its religious significance, Gangaramaya Temple plays an active role in the community. It hosts regular religious ceremonies, including chanting sessions and meditation retreats, offering spiritual guidance and solace to those seeking inner peace. The temple also actively engages in various charitable activities, such as providing education and healthcare services to underprivileged communities. One of the most notable events associated with Gangaramaya Temple is the annual Navam Perahera, a grand procession featuring traditional dancers, drummers, and beautifully adorned elephants.

Zoological Garden

The Zoological Garden Dehiwala, commonly known as Dehiwala Zoo, is a popular attraction located in Dehiwala, a suburb of Colombo, Sri Lanka. Established in 1936, it is the oldest and largest zoo in the country, spanning over 11 hectares (27 acres). The zoo is home to a diverse collection of animals, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and fish, making it a fascinating place for both locals and tourists to explore and learn about wildlife.
The Dehiwala Zoo offers visitors a unique opportunity to observe and interact with a wide variety of animal species from around the world. The zoo houses over 3,000 animals, representing more than 350 different species. Visitors can encounter majestic elephants, playful primates, graceful big cats, colorful birds, and fascinating reptiles. The zoo's well-designed enclosures aim to provide a natural habitat for the animals, allowing them to live comfortably and thrive.
Apart from its animal exhibits, Dehiwala Zoo offers additional attractions and facilities for visitors of all ages. The zoo features a walk-through aviary, where visitors can witness a diverse range of bird species up close. The aquarium showcases a variety of fish species, including vibrant tropical fish and unique marine creatures. The zoo also includes a butterfly garden, a reptile house, and a nocturnal animal section.

Nine Arches Bridge

The Nine Arches Bridge, also known as the Demodara Bridge, is an iconic railway viaduct located in Ella, Sri Lanka. Built during the British colonial era in the early 20th century, the bridge is renowned for its remarkable architecture and stunning natural surroundings. It spans a picturesque valley and stands at an impressive height, with nine arches that give it its name. The bridge is made entirely of bricks, stones, and cement, without the use of any steel. It is considered a marvel of engineering and a testament to the craftsmanship of that era.
The Nine Arches Bridge is not only an engineering marvel but also a popular tourist attraction. Visitors are drawn to its scenic beauty and the opportunity to witness a train crossing the bridge, which adds to the charm and creates a dramatic spectacle. The bridge is surrounded by lush green hills, tea plantations, and mist-covered landscapes, making it a favorite spot for photography enthusiasts and nature lovers.
The Nine Arches Bridge has become an integral part of the local community and serves as an important transportation link. Trains run regularly across the bridge, connecting the towns of Ella and Demodara. The bridge and the railway line are significant not only for local commuters but also for tourists who wish to experience the scenic train journey through the tea-covered hills of Sri Lanka's central highlands.

Ravana Falls

Ravana Falls, also known as Ravana Ella, is a magnificent waterfall located in Ella, Sri Lanka. The falls are named after the legendary King Ravana from the Hindu epic Ramayana. According to the myth, it is believed that King Ravana had his palace nearby and that his queen used to bathe in the pool formed by the cascading water. The waterfall is formed by the Ravana Oya, a tributary of the Kirindi Oya, and drops from a height of approximately 25 meters (82 feet).
Ravana Falls is not only a natural wonder but also a popular attraction for locals and tourists alike. The waterfall is easily accessible, with a short hike from the main road leading to a viewing platform where visitors can marvel at the cascading water and take in the breathtaking views. The mist created by the powerful waterfall creates a refreshing atmosphere, and visitors often enjoy cooling off in the natural pool formed at the base of the falls.
Apart from its natural allure, Ravana Falls holds cultural and historical significance. The waterfall is associated with various legends and folklore, adding a touch of mystique to its beauty. It is said to be one of the places where King Ravana's mythical palace was located, and the site attracts visitors interested in Sri Lanka's rich mythology and history. Additionally, the falls are part of the scenic landscape of Ella, a popular hill town known for its tea plantations, hiking trails, and stunning viewpoints.

Sigiriya

Sigiriya, also known as the Lion Rock, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic landmarks in Sri Lanka. Located in the central part of the country, Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress that dates back to the 5th century. Rising abruptly from the surrounding plains, the rock stands at a height of 200 meters (660 feet) and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The site is renowned for its historical and archaeological significance, as well as its remarkable blend of natural and man-made beauty.
The main attraction of Sigiriya is the ancient palace complex situated on top of the rock. Visitors can embark on a memorable journey to the summit by climbing steep staircases and narrow walkways that wind their way up the rock's side. Along the way, they encounter beautiful frescoes depicting celestial maidens known as "Sigiriya Damsels," which are considered some of the finest examples of ancient Sri Lankan art.
Aside from the palace complex, Sigiriya boasts other fascinating features. The beautifully landscaped gardens at the base of the rock are adorned with water gardens, reflecting pools, and terraced gardens, showcasing the advanced hydraulic engineering skills of the ancient civilization that inhabited the area. The Mirror Wall, adorned with ancient inscriptions and graffiti dating back centuries, offers a glimpse into the historical significance and cultural importance of Sigiriya.

Temple Of Tooth

The Temple of the Tooth, also known as Sri Dalada Maligawa, is a significant Buddhist temple located in Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is one of the most revered religious sites in the country and houses the sacred tooth relic of Lord Buddha. The temple's historical and religious importance dates back centuries, and it continues to be a major pilgrimage site for Buddhists from around the world.
The Temple of the Tooth is situated within the royal palace complex of Kandy, which was once the capital of the ancient Kingdom of Kandy. The temple's architecture showcases a blend of traditional Sri Lankan design elements and intricate craftsmanship. The entrance to the temple is guarded by a majestic moat and a large ornamental gate. Upon entering the temple, visitors are greeted with stunning murals depicting scenes from Buddhist mythology and history.
The highlight of the Temple of the Tooth is the chamber that houses the sacred tooth relic. The relic is believed to be the left upper canine tooth of Lord Buddha and is considered a precious spiritual artifact. It is securely stored in a golden casket adorned with precious gemstones. The tooth relic holds immense religious significance and is revered as a symbol of protection and prosperity for Sri Lanka. Devotees and visitors have the opportunity to pay their respects to the relic during daily rituals and special ceremonies held at the temple.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach is a popular coastal destination located in the southern part of Sri Lanka. Known for its pristine beauty and relaxed atmosphere, Unawatuna Beach attracts tourists and locals alike with its golden sands, clear turquoise waters, and palm-fringed shoreline. The beach stretches for about 4 kilometers (2.5 miles) and offers a variety of activities and amenities for visitors to enjoy.
Unawatuna Beach is renowned for its excellent swimming and snorkeling opportunities. The calm and shallow waters make it a safe haven for swimmers of all levels, including families with children. Snorkelers can explore the vibrant coral reefs located just a short distance from the shoreline, where they can encounter an array of colorful tropical fish and other marine life. Several local dive shops and tour operators provide snorkeling and diving excursions for those looking to explore the underwater wonders further.
The beach is lined with a wide range of restaurants, cafes, and beachside bars that cater to different tastes and preferences. Visitors can savor delicious seafood, traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, and international dishes while enjoying the scenic views of the ocean. In the evening, the beach comes alive with live music performances, beach parties, and a lively nightlife scene.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park, located in the southeastern part of Sri Lanka, is the country's most popular and second-largest national park. Spanning an area of approximately 979 square kilometers (378 square miles), Yala National Park is renowned for its diverse wildlife, breathtaking landscapes, and rich biodiversity.
Yala National Park is home to a wide range of animal species, including the elusive Sri Lankan leopard. It has one of the highest leopard densities in the world, making it a prime destination for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. Visitors can embark on thrilling safari adventures in open-top jeeps, guided by experienced naturalists who help spot and track these majestic big cats. The park is also home to elephants, sloth bears, spotted deer, wild boars, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species, making it a haven for nature lovers and birdwatchers.
The park's diverse ecosystems encompass dry forests, grasslands, lagoons, and coastal areas. This variety of habitats supports a rich array of plant and animal life. Yala National Park is particularly known for its picturesque landscapes, with dramatic rock formations, shimmering lakes, and golden savannahs. The park's iconic scenery, coupled with the excitement of spotting wildlife in its natural habitat, creates an unforgettable experience for visitors.
